Projects

  • Duration and Convexity of Bonds with Embedded Options
    Have you ever wondered how reliable are bond risk measures when your fixed-income investment is callable or putable? Standard bond risk measures of bonds with embedded options can be sensitive to changes in the underlying economic and financial conditions. This is because the value and behavior of these investment, can be affected by factors such as changes in interest rates, credit spreads, and volatility. As a result, bond risk measures for these types of bonds are difficult to predict and require sophisticated modeling techniques. The exact level of sensitivity will depend on the specifics of the bond, including the type and terms of the embedded options. I have developed a simple lattice-based short-rate model in VBA that calibrates and predicts the duration and convexity of bonds with embedded options.
